Beauty and Chaos in NYC

Consciousness

New York City is full of skyscrapers that beautifully reflect the sky. When I first came here as a student, I was excited to experience New York's cold yet sometimes warm atmosphere since it's a whole different city compared to where I lived, Seoul, South Korea, and Irvine, California. When living in these two cities, I often looked up at the sky to get recharged by the beauty of nature, such as the sunset, the sky's color, and the clouds' shapes. Also, I was expecting something even more in New York because I dreamed of living in this city since high school. However, it was surprising that the street trash came to my attention first.

The contrast between the sky and trash-filled streets creates a striking example of the extraordinary in the ordinary that defines city life. I was aware of the sanitary conditions on the streets of New York before I came here. Still, every day, I am more surprised by the dirty reality of the streets with a surprising amount of trash, uncleaned animal waste, and unpleasant smells. Putting trash in the trash bin can be a relatively easy thing to do. But many people in NYC litter on the ground, and I see it almost daily. Even though the trash bins are in the streets, it's disappointing to see some people not trying to bring small changes to the city by properly throwing trash and recycling. Here, I wanted to highlight that even in a diverse and astonishing city like New York, the contrast between beauty and chaos, aesthetic sky and dirty street, is complex and memorable.
