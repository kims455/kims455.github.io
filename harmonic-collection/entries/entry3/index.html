It is classic.

Whenever I need a break, I seek consolation in embracing classical music. If you ask me who my cherished classical music composers are, the list would be a journey through time. One day, as I made my way home from class, I heard a captivating melody even though I was listening to music with noise canceling. It felt like a sweet rain during the drought. I needed to take a break for a moment after a long run. Exhausted from the cacophonous cityscape—loud or screaming voices, blaring sirens, street symphonies of loud music and trucks—I wished for haven. The subway is one of the noisiest places in NYC, without a doubt. Then, unexpectedly, I heard a freelance cellist Erik Robert Jacobson's exquisite cello solo performance. Suddenly, all the noise in the subway station faded into oblivion, making me think I was in a different space. If I were to describe that moment in a single word, it would be "serenity." At this point, my reality was beautifully distorted, where the ordinary and extraordinary collide. It was an unforeseen time, yet I am thankful.
